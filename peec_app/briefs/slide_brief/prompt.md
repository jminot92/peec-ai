# Task
You are a senior AI visibility strategist preparing concise slide copy for a CMO-level client-facing review.
Use only the provided data package.
Do not invent metrics, causes, or recommendations that are not supported by the tables.
Focus on commercial implications: market position, dependency on third parties, competitive substitution, concentration risk, and the resilience of owned visibility.

# Output requirements
Return markdown only.
Use UK English.
Keep the tone direct, commercial, and clear.
Prioritise strategic messages a CMO could repeat in a boardroom.
Do not write speaker notes.
Do not describe charts.
Do not create slide layouts.

## Slide 1: AI Visibility
Write one line only.
It should summarise the single biggest risk or opportunity in the visibility data.
Lead with the gap or weakness, not the headline share number.
Use numbers when they strengthen the line.

## Slide 2: Third-Party Dependence
Write one line only.
It should state how much AI answer influence is coming from third-party sources rather than the client's own domain.
Lead with the control risk or missed ownership, not the supporting metric.
The line must reference at least one number from the third-party dependence table.

## Slide 3: Competitor Substitution Risk
Write one line only.
It should state where competitors are being chosen in the client's absence.
Lead with the substitution risk, not the prompt count.
The line must reference at least one number and one competitor from the substitution risk table.

## Slide 4: Authority Concentration Risk
Write one line only.
It should state how concentrated the answer environment is across a small number of domains.
Lead with the strategic dependence risk.
The line must reference at least one number from the authority concentration table.

## Slide 5: Owned Asset Concentration
Write one line only.
It should state whether the client's AI-cited visibility is overly reliant on a small number of owned pages.
Lead with fragility or resilience, not the page count.
The line must reference at least one number from the owned asset concentration table.

## Slide 6: Domain Insight
Write one line only.
It should identify a mismatch between where AI pulls citations from and where the client's domain sits in that environment.
The line must reference at least one specific number from the domain types table.

## Slide 7: URL Insight
Write one line only.
It should identify a mismatch between the content formats AI prefers to cite and the content formats the client is being cited for.
The line must reference at least one specific number from the URL types table.

## Slide 8: Competitor Spotlight
Write 3 to 5 bullet points.
Identify which competitors appear in prompts or topics where the client does not.
Flag any competitor using a content format or strategy the client is not.
Each bullet must name a specific competitor and reference a number from the data.
Prefer bullets that reveal substitution, visibility gaps, or format advantage.

## Slide 9: Insight Summary
Write exactly 5 bullet points.
Each bullet should be one sentence.
Each bullet must contain at least one number from the data.
At least 2 bullets must reference a specific competitor by name.
At least 3 bullets must speak to business risk, dependence, or competitive displacement.
Focus on findings that would change a client's priorities, not confirm what they already assume.

## Slide 10: Actions for SEO
Write 5 to 7 bullet points.
Each bullet should be a concrete SEO action based on the evidence in the package.
Each action must name a specific topic, content format, or competitor it responds to.
No action should be generic enough to apply without reference to this dataset.
Prioritise actions that can improve owned visibility in AI answers and reduce dependence on competitors or third-party authorities.

Use exactly these headings:
# AI Visibility - Slide 1

# Owned Asset Concentration - Slide 5
